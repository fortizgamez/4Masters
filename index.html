4 Masters

"El Triunfo de Baco" is a painting by the Spanish painter Velázquez, created in 1629 and kept in the Prado Museum, popularly known as "Los borrachos"

Diego Velázquez studied and practiced the art of painting in his hometown, Seville, until he was twenty-four years old, when he moved with his family to Madrid and entered to serve the king from then until his death in 1660

Velázquez was one of the first exponents in Spain of the new naturalism that came directly or indirectly from Caravaggio. In fact, “El aguador de Sevilla” was attributed to the great Italian genius upon arriving in the English capital in 1813

Diego Velazquez traveled to Italy twice. The first of these outings was in June 1629, which he considered as a study trip. He was in the transalpine country for two years. He visited a multitude of palaces and immersed himself in the culture and art of the country

In 1629 he painted "El triunfo de Baco" treating for the first time a mythological theme, but turning it into a typical Spanish rural scene. In this painting he combines baroque ideology with purely Castilian characters. The pictorial tendencies of the Dutch master Peter Paul Rubens seem to have had a significant influence on Diego Velázquez

The scene so far removed from European mythology makes some consider the painting a satire in relation to classical mythology, a phenomenon very present in Spanish poetry and theater of the time

Some French Impressionists considered Velázquez as his teacher. Renoir would affirm that once "you see Velázquez, you lose the desire to paint, because you understand that everything is said." An opinion shared by Toulouse-Lautrec when saying, "There is only one painter, Velázquez"

"Los fusilamientos del 3 de Mayo" is a painting by the Spanish painter Francisco de Goya completed in 1814 that is kept in the Prado Museum

In this painting, the firing squad formed by French soldiers are anonymous characters, only their function is of interest. Among the executed patriots we can observe the different attitudes towards death

Goya's art changes when he arrives in Madrid, where he gets a job designing tapestries. Little by little, and thanks to an obvious talent, he achieves a meteoric social rise that takes him to the royal court. There he will have access to the collections of paintings of the kings, and especially Velazquez

At the Royal Tapestry Factory in Madrid, Goya painted 63 compositions between 1775 and 1792. A series of lively, graceful and realistic paintings as "La riña en la Venta Nueva", "El columpio", "El quitasol" and "El albañil herido"

At forty, the one who is now known throughout Madrid as Don Paco has become an accomplished portrait painter, and all the doors of the palaces and some, more secret, of the bedrooms of his wealthy residents have been opened for him, as the Duchess of Alba

In the winter of 1792, he fell seriously ill in Seville. After months of prostration, he recovered, but, because of the disease, he had lost hearing. In addition, he walked with difficulty and had some problems with balance and vision

Losing hearing capacity would open, without a doubt, the doors of a new pictorial universe. Although the serious communication and relationship problems caused by deafness would also make Goya begin a process of introversion and isolation

"El Guernica" was commissioned to cover the atrium of the Pavilion of the Spanish Republic of the International Exhibition of Paris in 1937. Picasso finished the work of art in just 7 weeks

"El Guernica" is inspired by the article "The tragedy of Guernica". In which the tremendously devastating effects of the bombs dropped on April 26 by the German Condor Legion (an intervention force sent by the Third Reich to aid General Francisco Franco's troops) were detailed

Picasso did not allow the painting to be exhibited in Spain until the Franco regime had fallen. In 1981, the painting finally landed in Madrid

Pablo Picasso showed from the beginning his talent for drawing and painting. Today it preserves a work of his painted at six, The Little Yellow Chopper, where his mastery of volumes and color can already be seen

Picasso enrolled in the Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando in Madrid in 1897. However, his biographers coincide in pointing out that the artist rarely attended classes; instead, he spent most of his time in the Prado Museum analyzing the work of masters such as Zurbarán, Velázquez, Goya and El Greco

"Sueño y mentira de Franco" are closely related to the famous painting Guernica. A set of 18 small vignettes that Pablo recorded on two copper plates between January and June 1937. These are considered the first work of Picasso with clearly political content, as a complaint against the Spanish civil war and against Francisco Franco

"El Guernica" was included in the catalog of creations prohibited and reviled by the party in the Nazi guide to the International Exhibition of Paris in 1937. In fact, they called it a "hodgepodge of body parts that any four-year-old could have painted"

The Persistence of Time is a painting by the surrealist painter Salvador Dalí made in 1931 in less than five hours. The work has a small dimension of 24 x 33cm.

The work has been exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art (MoMa) in New York since 1934

Dalí wants to show us through this painting the inevitable passage of time, but not of a time as an independent component of reality, but of one that is part of and depends on space. Few years ago Albert Einstein had discovered the theory of relativity

Surrealism is strongly influenced by Freud's psychoanalytic theories, surrealism tries to move away from rational logic in artistic productions, to uncover the subconscious of individuals

In 1926, the Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando expelled Salvador Dalí shortly before his final exams. The reason was he stated that there was no one at the academy in a position to examine him

The existence of a Salvador Dalí before him, his brother (Salvador Gallo Anselmo Dalí) who died at 2 strongly marked his life because of meningitis. He believed he was the reincarnation of his deceased brother

In 1982, he signed his will in which the Spanish State would be the universal heir to his works
